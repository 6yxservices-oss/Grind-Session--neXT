**KEY CONTEXT:** Hurley started this season with only ~3 returning contributors from the NCAA Tournament team. Sports-Reference noted only 28.9% of minutes and 23.7% of scoring returned from 2022-23. This was a near-total roster rebuild.

**WIN SHARES RETAINED:** Very low. Lost the top scorer (Desmond Cambridge, 13.6 PPG), top rebounder/rim protector (Washington), and a starting wing (Devan Cambridge). Only Frankie Collins and Jamiya Neal returned as significant contributors.

**ASSESSMENT:** The transfer haul was mediocre. Jose Perez was a solid pickup, but the overall class lacked the impact of the prior year's Nevada pipeline. The team regressed from 23 wins to 14 wins.

| 2025-26 | 13-20 (24-25) | **Near 0% (1 scholarship returnee)** | Essentially none | Odum, Adams, Mukeba, Holiman, Trouet, international players | 17-16 |

**Pattern:** Hurley's portal strategy created a revolving door. After the 2022-23 NCAA Tournament team, ASU lost the majority of its production every single offseason and had to rebuild from scratch 3 years in a row. The inability to retain talent was the defining characteristic of the final Hurley years.

## 4. KEY TAKEAWAYS

### Transfer Portal Strategy Under Hurley
1. **Mid-major pipeline was the primary strategy:** The most successful transfers (Desmond Cambridge, Warren Washington, Alston Mason, BJ Freeman, Basheer Jihad) all came from mid-major programs (Nevada, Missouri State, Milwaukee, Ball State).

2. **Lateral Power conference transfers were hit-or-miss:** Adam Miller (LSU), Brandon Gardner (USC), Austin Nunez (Ole Miss) all underperformed or were non-factors at ASU.

3. **Retention was the fatal flaw:** ASU could not keep players. After every season, the majority of the roster entered the portal. The program had 3 consecutive near-complete roster overhauls (2023-24, 2024-25, 2025-26).

4. **Win shares cratered annually:** The 2022-23 NCAA Tournament team was built well through the portal, but Hurley could never sustain success because too many players left every spring.

5. **International pivot came too late:** The 2025-26 international class was the most ambitious international recruiting effort in program history, but it came in Hurley's final season with no institutional runway.

6. **Step-up transfers dominated:** The vast majority of incoming transfers were "step-up" moves from mid-major conferences (MWC, MVC, MAC, Horizon, WCC, Big West, Sun Belt, NAIA) to the Pac-12/Big 12. Very few Power conference lateral transfers were acquired.
